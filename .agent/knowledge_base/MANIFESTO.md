# The mo:os Manifesto: Sovereignty & Structure in the Age of AI

> **"Let corporate have their cookies and chatbots — We'll keep our tiny data."**

The artificial intelligence industry of 2026 is centralizing around two dangerous monopolies:

1. **The Monopoly of Memory:** Big Data corporations harvesting your interactions to train centralized, opaque models.
2. **The Monopoly of Execution:** "Agentic Frameworks" building brittle, proprietary workflows that lock you into specific providers and APIs.

We reject both. The goal of this project is to shatter these monopolies through two foundational pillars: **Data Sovereignty** and **Harness Agnosticism**.

---

## Pillar 1: Data Sovereignty (`my-tiny-data-collider`)

In the Big Data paradigm, you generate the data and corporations extract the value. You get a sanitized, generic chatbot in exchange for your personal context.

**my-tiny-data-collider** inverts this.
You control the data collider. You generate intelligence from your personal, curated knowledge domains.

- **YOUR Data:** Stored locally in your own verifiable graph database (PostgreSQL), not trapped in a cloud provider's vectors.
- **YOUR Rules:** Absolute privacy. Your memory never leaves your machine to train a corporate model.
- **YOUR Surplus:** The person closest to the data goldmine has the advantage. You own the metadata, the classifications, and the history of your computational thought.

We share the spirit of Tim Berners-Lee's Solid project: **Data must be decoupled from applications.** In our system, the UI is just a "Surface"—a temporary, interchangeable lens looking into your sovereign graph.

---

## Pillar 2: Harness Agnosticism (`mo:os`)

The current "agentic" landscape is a chaotic swamp of human constructs.

Concepts like "Skills," "Intents," "Chains-of-Thought," and "Agentic Workflows" are brittle buzzwords. They are arbitrary categories lacking absolute mechanical consensus. When 10,000 Model Context Protocol (MCP) tools are thrown into an exchange, combining them based on human semantic guessing results in unpredictable, un-composable chaos. The current hype assumes task decomposition is sufficient. It is not.

We need a universal, mathematical harness. **mo:os** is that operating system.

Instead of human constructs, `mo:os` relies on **Functorial Composition**.
Every API, every model, every tool, and every workflow is merely an object in a mathematical category: the **Container**.

- **Total Interchangeability:** We achieve complete independence of data processing. A Large Language Model is simply a CPU that we map our container schemas onto. If a provider degrades, you swap the model out, and the OS continues exactly as before.
- **Closed Geometry:** We do not rely on prompts to execute logic. The system mutates purely through four categorical morphisms (`ADD`, `LINK`, `MUTATE`, `UNLINK`). The structure guarantees the behavior.

## The Synthesis

**my-tiny-data-collider** gives you independence from data storage monopolies.  
**mo:os** gives you independence from data processing monopolies.

Together, they form a unified whole: A personal intelligence factory where the LLM is just a disposable engine, driving a mathematical harness over your sovereign knowledge graph.
