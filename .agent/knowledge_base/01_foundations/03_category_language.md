# Category Language

## Purpose

Provide the formal language for objects, morphisms, composition, categories, and related constructions.

## Canonicality

Canonical for category-theoretic interpretation.

## Seed scope

- object and morphism
- identity and composition
- category and subcategory
- full subcategory
- slice and coslice
- inclusion functor
- natural transformation
- functorial projection
- composed-function view of transition

## Composition rule

State-transition direction may be represented numerically or compositionally,
but the doctrine defines morphism meaning while contingent coordinates remain
outside this file.
